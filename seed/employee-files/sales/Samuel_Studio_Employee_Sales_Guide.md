SAMUEL STUDIO / EMPLOYEE SALES GUIDE 10\n\n## Page 11\n\nOBJECTION HANDLING
Acknowledge. Diagnose. Reframe.
Advance.
Do not argue with the objection. Find the concern underneath it and give the client a safe next step.
It is too expensive.
I understand. Is the concern the total budget, the timing of the payment, or whether the scope will create enough value? We can adjust the
scope if needed. I would rather remove features than reduce the quality of the build.
I can build this on Wix or Squarespace.
That can be a good DIY option. Samuel Studio is the fit when you want a custom direction, conversion-focused structure, integrations, and
support without spending your own time designing and troubleshooting the site.
I need to think about it.
Of course. What part still feels unresolved: the package, the investment, the timing, or confidence in the result? I can make the next step
clearer before we end the call.
I have a cheaper quote.
That makes sense. Compare the full scope: page count, custom design, mobile execution, SEO and analytics, integrations, content help,
launch support, and care after launch. The right comparison is what the quote will actually produce.
Do I have to pay everything upfront?
No. The site states that you can begin with a deposit or package payment. Let me confirm the approved payment structure for your project
before I promise specific terms.
Can I add features later?
Yes. You can start with the right core package and add SEO, booking, e-commerce, AI lead capture, or monthly support later. We should still
build the foundation with your likely next phase in mind.
Can you launch in seven days?
Some projects can launch in as little as seven days. I need to confirm the page count, content readiness, integrations, and approval timeline
before committing to a date.

Pricing and presentation
recommendations
The offer is strong and easy to understand. These refinements would make it easier for a new employee to quote consistently and for
prospects to compare confidently.
1. Add a true comparison strip
Show page range, SEO level, analytics, lead/CRM capability, support level, and ideal buyer in one row. This makes the upgrade logic visible
without reading every card.
2. Clarify the commerce boundary
The site promotes Sell Products or Services Online as an add-on, while Custom Website also names e-commerce stores. State that
straightforward checkout can be an add-on; complex catalogs, fulfillment, memberships, or integrations require a custom quote.
3. Define payment terms
The FAQ says deposit or package payment but does not state the standard deposit, milestone schedule, or when final payment is due. Publish
or internally standardize the approved structure.
4. Qualify the seven-day message
Keep the strong speed claim, but add the conditions: limited scope, content ready, timely approvals, and no complex integrations.
5. Make starting-price logic explicit
Place a short scope disclaimer beside package and add-on CTAs. This reduces surprise when content volume, product count, integrations, or
custom workflows change the quote.
6. Keep Professional as the anchor
The most-popular placement is strategically sound. Add a short 'choose this if' sentence that contrasts it directly with Starter and Growth.
7. Standardize care-plan limits
Define what counts as a minor content update, expected response windows, and any monthly hour or request limits for $49/month and
$100/month plans.
